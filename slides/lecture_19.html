Folk Classification (1959, 1962)

Key Concepts

  • Classification based on:
    • Grain types: Allochems (grains) like ooids, bioclasts, pellets, and intraclasts.
    • Matrix: Micrite (microcrystalline calcite) or Sparite (sparry calcite cement).

Components

  • Allochems: Framework grains.
    • Ooids: Spherical grains with concentric layers.
    • Bioclasts: Fragments of fossils.
    • Pellets: Rounded, fine-grained carbonate particles.
    • Intraclasts: Broken fragments of pre-existing carbonate rocks.
  • Matrix:
    • Micrite: Fine-grained lime mud.
    • Sparite: Coarse-grained crystalline cement.

Dunham Classification (1962)

Key Concepts

  • Emphasizes depositional texture and the proportion of grains to matrix.
  • Categories based on:

    • Depositional fabric: Original texture before diagenesis.

Comparison of Folk and Dunham Systems

Aspect Folk Dunham
Focus Composition: grains and matrix. Texture: depositional fabric and grain.
Key Terms Micrite, Sparite, Allochems. Mudstone, Wackestone, Packstone, etc.
Application Petrological and geochemical studies. Sedimentological and environmental analysis.

Examples of Carbonate Rocks

Rock Type Description Classification
Oolitic Limestone Dominated by ooids; grain-supported. Folk: Oosparite; Dunham: Grainstone.
Fossiliferous Limestone Contains abundant fossils. Folk: Biosparite; Dunham: Wackestone or Packstone.
Micritic Limestone Fine-grained lime mud. Folk: Micrite; Dunham: Mudstone.
