Systems

What Is a System?

A system is the part of the universe under study, such as a magma chamber or metamorphic rock.

Surroundings

The surroundings include everything outside the system that can exchange energy or matter with it.

Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work or cause change. All natural processes involve energy transfer.

Types of Systems

System Type Matter Exchange Energy Exchange Example
Open Yes Yes Volcano releasing lava and gas
Closed No Yes Sealed magma chamber
Isolated No No Ideal thermos (hypothetical)

Types of Energy in Systems

  • Thermal energy: due to temperature
  • Chemical energy: stored in bonds
  • Potential energy: related to position
  • Kinetic energy: motion (minor in rocks)
  • Radiative energy: from radioactive decay

Stability and Energy

  • Stable = lowest energy state (a rock sitting on the ground).
  • Unstable = wants to change right away (a rock in mid-air).
  • Metastable = seems stable, but could change if disturbed (like diamonds, which should turn into graphiteâbut donât, because of energy barriers).

Natural systems tend to move toward the lowest energy state.

Gibbs Free Energy (G)

It tells us whether a mineral or rock will be stable under certain conditions.

The equation is:

\[ G = H - T \times S \]

  • \(H\): Enthalpy (heat content)
  • \(S\): Entropy (disorder)
  • \(T\): Temperature (K)
  • \(\Delta G < 0\): spontaneous
  • \(\Delta G = 0\): equilibrium

Enthalpy (H)

Total heat content of the system. Reactions with ÎH < 0 release heat.

Entropy (S)

Degree of disorder.
Solids = low S, gases = high S.

Energy Levels

  • High energy: system is reactive, unstable
  • Low energy: system is more stable
